Taras Shevchenko

Taras Hryhorovych Shevchenko (1814-1861) was a Ukrainian poet, artist, and political figure born into serfdom. Despite facing hardships, his talent in drawing and writing was recognized, leading to his enrollment at the St. Petersburg Academy of Fine Arts. Shevchenko's collection of poetry, "Kobzar," published in 1840, established him as a key figure in Ukrainian literature, using his works to critique social injustice and advocate for the liberation of his people. His enduring legacy lies in his role as a national symbol of Ukrainian identity, with his poetry and art continuing to inspire generations.
